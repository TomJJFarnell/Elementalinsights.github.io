Recent Dashboards

Here are some real dashboards I've built to solve problems for local organisations — each with measurable results.

1. Community Food Bank – Demand & Forecasting

Client: London-based food hub supporting over 100 families per month.

Challenge: The team was tracking food parcel numbers and donations in spreadsheets, but couldn’t easily see trends or plan ahead.

Solution: I created a dynamic dashboard that shows parcel demand by month, household size, and source of donations. It also includes a simple forecast of demand peaks (e.g. winter holidays).

Outcome: Helped volunteers plan restocks more efficiently, avoid mid-month shortages, and improve reporting to their grant funder.

2. School Science Department – Chemical Safety Inventory

Client: Secondary school science department with over 800 chemical items in stock.

Challenge: Manual inventory updates were time-consuming, and there was no visibility on expired or hazardous stock levels.

Solution: Built an interactive inventory dashboard showing current stock by hazard class, expiry status, and storage location. Added GHS pictograms and search filtering.

Outcome: Reduced inventory management time by over 60% and helped the department pass their annual safety audit with no flagged issues.

3. Online Retail – Sales & Funnel Performance

Client: Small e-commerce shop selling educational print books on Amazon and Shopify.

Challenge: Sales data was split across multiple platforms, and there was no clear view of which product categories or time periods performed best.

Solution: Merged sales data into a unified dashboard showing top-performing products, seasonal trends, and ROI by marketplace.

Outcome: Helped the owner focus ad spend on high-margin titles and plan stock orders ahead of seasonal spikes, increasing Q4 profit by 18%.

4. NHS Forecasting – Outpatient Attendances & DNA Rates

Client: Public health project using NHS open data from 2011–2020.

Challenge: NHS Trusts needed a simple way to visualise hospital-level attendance, missed appointments (DNAs), and referrals over time.

Solution: Built an interactive Power BI report with filters by hospital, department, and year — showing trends, heatmaps, and forecasted demand.

Outcome: Enabled better scheduling policy research, and highlighted where GP referrals dropped during COVID lockdowns.
